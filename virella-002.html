A partially bio-based thermoplastic polyimide for industrial 3D printing. Virella-002 delivers equal strength in every direction, withstands temperatures beyond 225°C, and enables production-grade parts in aerospace, defense, and industrial applications.

Material Overview

The Z-strength problem in 3D printing — solved.

The 3D printing industry has long faced a critical limitation: printed parts are weaker in the Z-axis than in the XY plane. For applications in aerospace, defense, and industrial manufacturing, this anisotropy makes today's high-performance polymers unsuitable for production-grade components.

Virella-002 is engineered to close this gap. Its novel polyimide architecture enables bonding strength in all print directions that is equal — true isotropy — while maintaining the high-temperature performance required by the most demanding end applications. The result is a material that makes 3D-printed parts genuinely suitable for real-world engineering duty cycles.

Key Properties
01
True Isotropic Strength — Equal mechanical performance in X, Y, and Z axes after printing. No weak layer interfaces.
02
High Temperature Resistance — Heat deflection temperature of 167–175°C (ISO 75), Tg of 195°C. Suitable for extended high-heat environments.
03
Inherent Flame Retardancy — Passes flame retardancy requirements without halogenated additives — essential for aerospace qualification.
04
Excellent Electrical Insulation — High dielectric strength and low dielectric loss make Virella-002 suitable for demanding electrical and electronic applications.
